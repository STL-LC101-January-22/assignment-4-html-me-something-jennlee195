Struggle Meals

My journey into cooking started where I think it does for a lot of people -- college.
It was my second year to be exact. I was finally living in an apartment with a full kitchen and no longer had a meal plan that would allow me off to survive off of dining hall chicken tendies. Armed with a functioning burner, a pot and pan, and a very, very limited budget, (I'll forever be amazed at how far I could make an extra 99¢ on groceries go back then) I was ready to survive on my own. It was during this time I learned to make staples like lentil soup, rice, roast chicken, and spaghetti.

Something in Between

Around the time I had finished learning a the basics, Buzzfeed's Tasty platform absolutely took off.
My mother had FoodNetwork, I now had Tasty. Cooking tutorials had never been so consumable (no pun intended) and seeing a steak dinner for two be broken down into a seamlessly edited 2-minute clip with cheery elevator music made me feel like I could now cook anything I could find aa video for. At the same time, I had just turned 21 and had begun my career working in bars. My new income from tips would be going straight to Schnucks & Aldi.

Farm to Table, but Make it #Fancy

A few years ago I met my boyfriend who made me think about my cooking in an entirely different way by asking me one question on our first date: Had I ever cooked with wild game?
My answer was no, but as a hippie millennial I'd always been a fan of the Farm to Table concept. Having grown up in Hawaii, sustainability is very close to my heart and what better way could I live off the land than harvest my own meat? And now with years of cooking under my belt, how fancy could I dress up wild game? And this is where I am today -- continually finding new and fun ways to use the deer meat we get from hunting every year.
